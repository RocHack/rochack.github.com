DandyHacks Spring '15

RocHack's second 43 hour hackathon.

Open to all students at all schools starting on April 3rd at 5pm and ending the 5th at noon. Rettner Hall at the University of Rochester.

Register now

A hacka-what?

Hackathon! It's an event about building the coolest thing you can in 36 hours and meeting awesome people. We don't place any limits on what you can build. Traditionally there are software projects but we'd love to see hardware, or whatever else people come up with as well. It just has to be built within the 36 hours.

At the end of the 36 hours we have judges get together and determine the coolest hacks. Winners get awesome prizes!

When and where?

April 3rd 5pm to April 5th at noon, Rettner Hall (the new building right next to Wilson Commons) at the University of Rochester.

If you're coming from a different school and need help with parking, getting here, or anything else, email us. We're more than happy to help with anything!

FAQ

Do I bring anything?

Yep! Bring a laptop and anything else you need to get you into the coding zone. If you're from out of town and want to sleep make sure you bring a comfy sleeping bag!

If you're interested in a hardware hack, bring along some equipment but we will have plenty of toys for you to use in your project if you need anything!

Do I have pay to attend?

No! The hackathon is completely free to all students who want to attend. The only thing you'll have to cover is transportation (if you're coming from other places) and some food.

Will there be food?

Yes! We're still working out the details but we'll at least cover one or two of your meals while you're hacking. Worst case, if you're still hungry, we are 3 minutes away from The Pit (our a-la-carte dining hall in Wilson Commons).

What if I don't have an idea?

Come anyways! We can help you find a team that already has an idea or we'll have a list of ideas from departments across campus that you can browse through. Regardless, we'll figure something awesome out for you!

What if I have a cool idea but don't know how to code?

If you're interested in learning how to program, you should totally come. Hackathons are a great way to get started with building your awesome idea for yourself! If coding isn't for you, we have a form for outside ideas that anyone at the hackathon can build if they don't have their own idea.

Any other questions?

Get in touch! You can contact us via email and we'll get back to you as soon as possible.
